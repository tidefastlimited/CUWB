# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

CUWB serves technical buying committees evaluating high-precision real-time location systems. Primary users include industrial operations teams, engineering and robotics teams, systems integrators, OEMs, healthcare asset teams, and sports or experience teams that need fast, local position data.

These users are usually comparing technologies, estimating deployment complexity, justifying cost, and checking whether the system can integrate with existing software or infrastructure. They need clarity before persuasion.

## Product Purpose

The site exists to make CUWB the clearest online source for buyers who want high-precision UWB RTLS without recurring CUWB software licensing or cloud dependency. It should explain what CUWB is, where it fits, how it compares, what it costs to deploy, and why Ciholas engineering depth matters.

Success means buyers can quickly understand whether CUWB fits their use case, estimate the practical requirements of deployment, trust the technical claims, and move toward a product evaluation or deployment-planning conversation.

## Brand Personality

Precise, transparent, engineer-led, polished.

CUWB should feel like a serious RTLS company built by engineers for buyers making expensive operational decisions, with the polish of a modern infrastructure startup. The tone is factual, specific, and confident. The brand should communicate control, technical competence, commercial honesty, and product maturity without becoming cold or academic.

## Anti-references

Do not make CUWB look like a generic enterprise SaaS platform, a glossy industrial brochure, a hardware startup splash page, or an academic lab site. Avoid blue startup palettes, gradient heroes, decorative icon grids, vague “platform” language, oversized marketing cards, and empty claims without proof. Polished should mean mature and premium, not generic startup gloss.

The design should not over-index on rugged industrial grit. CUWB sells technical confidence and deployment clarity, not construction-site theater.

## Design Principles

1. **Transparency is the interface.** Pricing, tradeoffs, deployment needs, best-fit guidance, and not-fit guidance should be easy to find and easy to compare.
2. **Technical proof beats broad claims.** Use specs, diagrams, deployment examples, tables, anchor counts, latency, update rates, API details, and TCO examples wherever possible.
3. **One system, multiple customer modes.** The core language is precise and technical, while industry pages can flex: industrial pages are operational, engineering pages are data-forward, healthcare pages are calmer and trust-led, sports pages can show more motion.
4. **Red is a signal, not wallpaper.** CUWB red should mark action, emphasis, and measurement, not flood every surface.
5. **Buyer clarity comes before brand drama.** The site can be visually distinct, but every layout choice must help a buyer scan, compare, justify, or act.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain strong contrast across light and dark modes, do not rely on color alone for status or comparison, support reduced motion, and keep dense technical content readable with clear hierarchy and predictable navigation.
